Arts/Humanities

The Humanities stream in high school focuses on subjects that explore human culture, society, and thought. It encompasses areas like history, literature, philosophy, languages, and social sciences. It develops critical thinking, communication, and analytical skills, preparing students for diverse career paths.

Subjects You'll Study:

1. Political Science
What it is:
It deals with how governments work, political theories, power, and policies. You'll learn about Indian and global politics.
What you’ll learn:
  • Indian Constitution and Government
  • Rights and Duties of citizens
  • Political ideologies (liberalism, socialism, etc.)
  • International relations and organizations (UN, WTO)
  • Electoral systems, democracy, and political parties
Why it matters:
Useful for UPSC, law, political journalism, and international relations careers.
2. History
What it is:
Study of past events – kings, empires, revolutions, wars, cultures, and movements that shaped today’s world.
What you’ll learn:
  • Ancient, Medieval, and Modern Indian history
  • World history (Industrial revolution, World Wars)
  • Nationalism, freedom movements
  • Social and cultural changes in India
Why it matters:
Helpful for UPSC, teaching, research, museum work, or archaeology.
3. Psychology
What it is:
The scientific study of human mind and behavior. It explains how people think, feel, and act.
What you’ll learn:
  • Learning and memory
  • Emotions and motivation
  • Personality and intelligence
  • Psychological disorders and therapy
  • Practicals based on observation and analysis
Why it matters:
Great for careers in psychology, counselling, HR, mental health, or therapy.
4. Geography
What it is:
Study of Earth, environment, and how humans interact with nature.
What you’ll learn:
  • Physical Geography (landforms, climate, soils)
  • Human Geography (population, migration, settlements)
  • Resources and industries
  • Maps, diagrams, and data analysis
Why it matters:
Useful in civil services, urban planning, GIS, environmental studies, etc.
5. Sociology
What it is:
Study of society, culture, institutions, and social behavior.
What you’ll learn:
  • Social structure and social change
  • Family, caste, religion, and education
  • Gender and inequality
  • Globalization and social movements
Why it matters:
Good for social work, journalism, civil services, NGOs, or research.
6. English
What it is:
Focus on improving your language, comprehension, and literary skills.
What you’ll learn:
  • Reading comprehension
  • Writing formats (articles, letters, debates)
  • Literature: prose, poems, plays
  • Grammar and vocabulary
Why it matters:
Essential for communication-based careers and all entrance exams (CUET, UPSC, IELTS, etc.)
7. Optional Subjects (based on availability)
  • Mathematics: Useful for economics, statistics, competitive exams.
  • Economics: For understanding markets, policies, and budgets – good for finance, civil services, or business.
  • Informatics Practices (IP): Programming, data handling – useful for digital careers.
  • Fine Arts / Painting: Great for creative fields like design, animation, or architecture.
  • Home Science: Nutrition, human development, resource management – useful for health or teaching careers.
  • Music / Dance / Theatre: For performance arts and creative careers.
