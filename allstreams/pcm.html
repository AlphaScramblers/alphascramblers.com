Science Non-Medical(PCM)

The Science stream with a non-medical focus is chosen by students interested in engineering, technology, or physical sciences.It includes Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics, but does not include Biology. This path develops logical reasoning, analytical skills, and a strong foundation in scientific and mathematical principles.

Subjects You'll Study:

1.Physics
What it is:
Physics is the science of matter, motion, energy, and forces. It helps us understand how the universe behaves — from atoms to galaxies. What you'll learn
  • Kinematics and Laws of Motion
  • Work, Energy, and Power
  • Thermodynamics and Heat
  • Optics, Sound, Waves
  • Electricity, Magnetism, and Modern Physics
Why it matters:
Crucial for engineering, architecture, data science, and careers in technology and physical sciences.
2.Chemistry
What it is:
Chemistry is about substances and how they interact, combine, and change. It connects physics with biology and is key to materials and processes. What you'll learn
  • Physical Chemistry: Mole Concept, Thermodynamics
  • Inorganic Chemistry: Periodic Table, Bonding, Coordination Compounds
  • Organic Chemistry: Hydrocarbons, Functional Groups, Reactions
Why it matters:
Important for engineering, pharmacy and chemistry research.
3.Mathematics
What it is:
Mathematics in PCM is advanced and logic-oriented — useful for problem-solving, modelling, and analysis. What you'll learn
  • Sets, Relations & Functions
  • Algebra: Quadratic Equations, Complex Numbers
  • Calculus: Limits, Derivatives
  • Trigonometry and Coordinate Geometry
  • Statistics & Probability
  • Vectors & 3D Geometry
Why it matters:
Essential for engineering, computer science, finance, and data-oriented careers. Required for JEE, NDA, CUET, etc.
4.English
What it is:
Core language subject that strengthens communication, comprehension, and formal writing skills What you'll learn
  • Reading Comprehension
  • Grammar and Vocabulary
  • Writing Skills (letters, essays, reports)
  • Literature: Poetry, Stories, Drama
Why it matters:
Important for professional communication, interview skills, and exams like CUET, UPSC, IELTS
5.Optional Subjects (choose one, if offered):
  • Computer Science/Informatics Practices – Learn programming (Python, Java), databases, data structures
  • Economics – Micro and macroeconomics, stats, and policy
  • Physical Education – Sports, health science, and fitness
  • Engineering Graphics – Technical drawing for engineering design
  • Statistics & Probability
  • Vectors & 3D Geometry
